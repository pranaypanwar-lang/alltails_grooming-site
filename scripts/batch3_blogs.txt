## Why Shih Tzu grooming needs special attention

Shih Tzus have long, fast-growing coats that can mat around the ears, legs, belly, tail and underarms if not groomed regularly. Their face and eye area also need gentle hygiene attention.

Common grooming needs for Shih Tzu include:

- Bathing and conditioning
- Blow drying
- Face trimming
- Hygiene trimming
- Under-paw trimming
- Ear cleaning
- Nail trimming
- Teddy cut or summer cut
- Dematting if knots are present

Regular grooming helps your pet feel cleaner and more comfortable while also making coat issues easier to notice early.

## Best haircut styles for Shih Tzus

Popular Shih Tzu grooming styles include teddy cut, summer cut and neat breed-style trimming.

A teddy cut keeps the face rounded and soft while making the body coat easier to manage. A summer cut keeps the coat shorter and more practical during hot weather. The final style should depend on coat condition, matting and pet-parent preference.

## Why Shih Tzus get matted easily

Shih Tzus can develop knots when the coat is not brushed or dried properly. Mats often form behind ears, around the collar area, under the legs, near the belly and around the tail. If matting is present, choose Complete Pampering because dematting is included.
